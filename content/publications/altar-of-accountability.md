abstract: "This book examines the institutional role of religion in Nigeria and its relationship to governance, accountability, and social justice. Drawing on political economy and institutional analysis, it interrogates whether organized religion in Sub-Saharan Africa fulfils its social contract, or whether the absence of accountability mechanisms within religious institutions undermines broader civic values. The work situates religious institutions within frameworks of public-interest governance and economic development."
