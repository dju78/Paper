abstract: This paper examines how small and medium-sized enterprises (SMEs) in
  the United Kingdom encounter automated decision-making and profiling through
  routine business software such as payroll, recruitment, and payment systems.
  Using qualitative regulatory analysis and practitioner observation, it
  evaluates the operational application of Article 22 of the UK GDPR and
  identifies compliance gaps created by vendor opacity, limited audit
  visibility, and the absence of structured human review processes. The paper
  proposes proportionate compliance tools, vendor accountability expectations,
  and practical review procedures to improve transparency, accountability, and
  protection of data subject rights.
